Organic food is just a trendy scam.

Organic fruits and vegetables require more land and energy to equal conventional yields. Lesser use of pesticides and herbicides make crops more vulnerable to attacks. Decreasing the use of synthetic sprays and chemicals require more crop health maintenance.


Natural fertilizers like manure have unregulated nitrogren release. This leads to acidification, the leaching of excess nutrients in bodies of water. Surrounding soil and water run-offs into nearby lakes and rivers increase pH levels, which change the soil and water quality.

While comparing Omega-3 fatty acids in organic and conventional food products, insufficient evidence does not prove to say organic food is outright better. But having more of these Omega-3s is beneficial for cardiovascular health.


The rising buzzword “antioxidants”, which present some food as being very healthy choices for eating. As organic foods are labelled healthier and it is true they they contain more. But there is still debate on the idea of increasing antioxidant consumption will increase an individual’s overall health.

Organic farming systems are not banned from using pesticides. Presence of the chemicals should be restricted at the discretion of a farm. To keep the integrity of organic practices, laws regulate the amount and type of chemicals used, but not the full removal of pesticides from crops.


While organic produce may report lesser traces of pesticide residue, these are still toxins. Not to say that we should go banning all sprays, samples are tested regularly and are kept within tolerances. These pesticides protect crops from disaster attacks and diseases that can destroy a whole season of produce.
